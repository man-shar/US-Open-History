A brief history of the US Open

By Manas Sharma

10 September,2017

The Open Era starts

“Rocket” Rod Laver from Australia completes his second calendar-year Grand Slam and remains the only man in the Open Era to achieve it.

Ken Rosewall from Australia defeated Tony Roche in the final. This was Roche’s second conseutive loss in the final.

It was the first Grand Slam tournament in which the tiebreak was introduced.

USA’s Stan Smith won the final against Czech player Jan Kodes. This was his first of two Grand Slams.

The US Open was held on grass back then.

Arthur Ashe won his first and only US Open. He is still the only black man to win it.

96 players players in the first US Open. 51 of them were from USA.

Frank Parker at 52, still holds the record for the oldest man to compete in a Grand Slam singles tournament

Romanian Ilie Nastase defeated Arthur Ashe in the final.

The Amritraj brothers played their first US Open

John Newcombe from Australia defeated Arthur Ashe in the final.

Vijay Amritraj reached the Quarter Final which is the best peformance by an Indian single’s player at the US Open. He lost to Ken Rosewall.

American Jimmy Connors defeated Ken Rosewall in the shortest Grand Slam final ever played.

Vijay Amritraj reached his second consecutive Quarter Final and lost yet again to Rosewall.

Spain’s Manuel Orantes defeated Jimmy Connors in the final.

The surface was changed to green coloured clay after complaints from the players about the uneven bounce on grass.

Jimmy Connors defeated Sweden’s Bjorn Borg in the final.

This year saw the beginning of USA’s domination at the US Open. Both in terms of number of players and champions.

Argentinian Guillermo Vitas defeated Connors in the final. Vitas went on to set the third longest winning streak - 46 matches -ending at the Paris Masters in 1977.

This was the last year the US Open was played on clay.

Jimmy Connors defeated Borg in a repeat of the 1976 final.

This was the first time the Open was played on hard courts at the legendary Flushing Meadows.

The fiery American John McEnroe defeated Vitas Gerulaitis in the final to win his first US Open.

During the second round match between Nastase and McEnroe, the umpire fouled Nastase a point in third set and then a game in the fourth for arguing and stalling. A near riot ensued as the crowd showed its disagreement by throwing beer cans on court. The match was eventually restarted with the umpire being replaced before McEnroe finally won.

McEnroe defeated Borg in the final in five sets in what is regarded as one of the greatest matches ever played. They were known as "Fire and Ice" due to their contrasting personalities on the court.

This was the 100th edition of the US Open.

McEnroe became the first male player since 1920s to win three consecutive US Open titles. He defeated Borg in four sets a repeat of last year's final. This was Borg's last Grand Slam match.

McEnroe also won the Men's Doubles with Peter Fleming this year.

This year also saw the beginning of the decline in India's representation at major tennis tournaments. Only R Krishnan played from India, reaching the Quarter Finals.

Jimmy Connors defeated Czech Ivan Lendl in the final. It was Connors's 7th career Grand Slam title and his 4th US Open title.

In a rematch of last year's final, Jimmy Connors defeated Czech Ivan Lendl. It was Connors's second consecutive and overall 5th US Open title.

This year marked the beginning of the rise of European players at the US Open. Led by Sweden and France, many newer nations like Germany, Italy and Czech Republic began producing great players.

McEnroe won his 4th US Open title, defeating Ivan Lendl who suffered his third consecutive defeat in the final.

This year marked the beginning of the rise of European players at the US Open. Led by Sweden and France, many newer nations like Germany, Italy and Czech Republic began producing great players.
